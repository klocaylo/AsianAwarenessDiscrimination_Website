How to Support the Asian American Community

Asian American discrimination is nothing new to American society. The prejudice and racism that they experience is often something that cannot be defeated with just one community. It is up to the Asian American community, and the other communities within the U.S., to stand up against this discrimination and put an end to it. As a person of the Asian American community, or as an ally, there are several actions that you can do to help support the Asian American community.

1. Refrain from calling COVID-19 the "Chinese Virus"

As many people know, COVID-19 originated from Wuhan, China, hence why several people call the virus the "Chinese Virus". While it may seem accurate and intuitive to refer to a virus based on its origin, doing so causes more harm than good. Connecting a disease to its place of origin has been known to lead to discrimination.

It is because of this reason that the World Health Organization (WHO) has recommended that the virus be referred to as COVID-19. By doing this, people will start to view this as an "Us vs. Virus" situation instead of as a "Us vs. Them" situation. It is a very simple action to do, but it is one that will make a big difference.

2. Support Asian Businesses

Over the last few months many businesses and restaurants have been hurt due to COVID-19 and the quarantine period needed to recover from the pandemic. However, because of the panic surrounding the virus and its origins, Asian businesses, especially Asian restaurants, having been negatively impacted the most. These restaurants are losing business and are struggling to stay afloat with many of them losing 50% or even 80% of their business.

In order to help these businesses support themselves and its employees, especially during a time when unemployment is at an all time high, you can simply eat at your favorite Asian restaurant or buy items from an Asian owned business. Not only will this help the business and its employees to have income for their needs during this time, but it will also display to them that you support them.

3. Get Involved

Getting involved can be as easy as spreading the word on social media or as large as joining a protest. No matter how big or small your involvement is, it's important to simply be involved and be a voice for those who don't have one.

There are so many things you can do to help support the Asian American community. You can join a group that's advocating for Asian American Rights, become more educated regarding the struggles and the realities of the community, go volunteering, and listen to the stories of those in the Asian American community itself.
